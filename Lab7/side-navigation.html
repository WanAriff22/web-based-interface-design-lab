Section 1

A Formula One car or F1 car is a single-seater, open-cockpit, open-wheel formula racing car used to compete in Formula One racing events.

Section 2

Early F1 cars were simpler in design with no wings, front mounted engines. These cars also required significant driver effort for control.

Section 3

Modern F1 cars are composed of carbon fibre monocoques with open cockpits consisting of a single driver seat and detachable steering. An F1 power unit, a 1.6 L turbo hybrid V6 engine.
